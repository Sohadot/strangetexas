The Story Behind the Stories

StrangeTexas

Some names carry a question inside them.

Strange Texas is one of those names.

When we first encountered it, we did not choose it so much as it chose us. There was something in the pairing of those two words, Strange and Texas, that felt like an open door. Not a door to horror or cheap mystery, but a door to something older and more honest. A door to the kind of stories that real places accumulate over centuries, quietly, without asking for attention.

Texas is not like other places. This is not sentiment. It is geography, history, and something harder to name. A state so large it contains its own climates, its own cultures, its own silences. A place where the distance between towns can feel like the distance between worlds. Where the land itself seems to remember things that the people living on it have long since forgotten.

Some places earn their strangeness cheaply, through rumor and exaggeration. Texas earned its differently. Through a history that was genuinely violent and genuinely vast. Through landscapes that do not comfort the eye so much as challenge it. Through a character so particular that it resists every attempt to reduce it to something simple or familiar.

Strange Texas was built on a simple belief. That the most interesting stories are not invented. They are found. In old houses that refuse to stay quiet. In desert highways where the night does its own impossible things. In bayous that suddenly glow blue after dark. In legal codes that preserved the ghost of a world that no longer exists.

We are not here to frighten you. We are not here to convince you of anything. We are here because some places earn their mystery honestly, over generations, and Texas has been earning its for a very long time.

This is where we keep the
stories it left behind.
